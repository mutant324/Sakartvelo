  • GEORGIA

    Georgia bridges continents, where the Greater Caucasus Mountains hold traces of prehistoric people, and ancient wines and frescoes evoke medieval empires' golden age. Tbilisi, with its sulfur baths and winding streets, embodies the hospitality and resilience of a people enduring Persian, Arab, Mongol, Ottoman, and Russian conquests. Architecture is iconic: Vardzia's 12th-century frescoes (underground monastic city) and UNESCO-listed Svan towers. Khorumi dances (warlike) and panduri/chonguri music form core identity. In 2025, culture is pitched as "universal heritage": three UNESCO sites (Gelati, Mtskheta, Upper Svaneti), plus Getty Foundation restoration grants. Cuisine shines: khachapuri, khinkali, and qvevri wines (8,000-year tradition, UNESCO 2013) exported worldwide.
